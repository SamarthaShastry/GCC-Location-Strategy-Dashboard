# Limitations & Assumptions

## Data Limitations
- All underlying data sourced from a single report (Deloitte-NASSCOM, 
  August 2023) — subject to the limitations of that dataset
- Data represents a point-in-time snapshot — city conditions evolve 
  and scores may shift with updated data
- 20 cities covered — excludes several emerging GCC locations and all 
  Tier-1 cities

## Methodological Limitations
- Min-max normalization is sensitive to outliers — one exceptional 
  city can compress scores of all others
- Equal weighting within pillars assumes all constituent metrics are 
  equally important — this may not hold in all cases
- Composite scores are relative indices — a score of 49 does not 
  mean 49% of maximum possible attractiveness

## Missing Metrics
The following strategically relevant metrics were not available in 
the source dataset and are not reflected in scores:
- Talent attrition rates by city
- Government incentive packages and tax breaks
- English language proficiency index
- Existing GCC density and market saturation
- Power infrastructure reliability
- Political stability index
- University tie-up potential
- Real estate availability (beyond rental cost)

## Disclaimer
This dashboard is an independent analytical tool and does not 
represent the views of Deloitte, NASSCOM or any other organisation. 
All recommendations should be validated through on-ground due 
diligence before finalisation.
