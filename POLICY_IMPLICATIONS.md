# Policy Implications

## Key Findings

### 1. South India Dominates GCC Attractiveness
Cities such as Mangaluru, Thiruvananthapuram, Coimbatore and Tirupati 
consistently rank in the top tier across multiple priority 
configurations. This reflects a convergence of talent density, cost 
efficiency and liveability that North and Central Indian cities have 
yet to replicate.

**Implication:** State governments in North and Central India should 
prioritise talent pipeline development, SEZ expansion and EoDB 
improvements to compete for GCC investments.

### 2. Infrastructure Remains the Critical Gap
Nine of 20 cities have zero metro connectivity. One city has no 
commercial flights. Infrastructure deficits disproportionately affect 
otherwise attractive cities like Thiruvananthapuram and Mangaluru.

**Implication:** Central and state government investment in 
last-mile connectivity — metro expansion, airport capacity and 
broadband infrastructure — would materially improve GCC 
attractiveness scores for multiple cities simultaneously.

### 3. Cost Advantage is Eroding in Top Cities
Cities ranking highest on talent availability (Thiruvananthapuram, 
Bhubaneswar) are beginning to show rising salary expectations as 
GCC demand increases. The cost advantage that makes Tier-2 cities 
attractive is not permanent.

**Implication:** Early movers into high-potential Tier-2 cities will 
capture the cost advantage before salary inflation catches up. 
Organisations should treat location selection as a time-sensitive 
decision.

### 4. Ecosystem Depth is Underdeveloped Outside Tech Clusters
Tech ecosystem scores remain low across most Tier-2 cities relative 
to Tier-1 counterparts. Vendor depth, innovation infrastructure and 
university-industry linkages are nascent.

**Implication:** GCCs entering Tier-2 markets should factor in 
ecosystem development costs and timelines. Partnering with local 
universities and industry bodies from day one is critical to building 
a sustainable talent and vendor ecosystem.

### 5. Liveability is an Underweighted Decision Factor
Most GCC location frameworks overweight talent and cost while 
underweighting liveability. This analysis shows that cities with 
strong liveability scores (Mangaluru, Nashik) demonstrate lower 
attrition risk — a factor that compounds into significant cost 
savings over time.

**Implication:** Organisations should explicitly weight liveability 
in their location framework, particularly for senior and specialised 
roles where retention is critical.

## Recommendations for Policymakers
- Develop Tier-2 city GCC readiness indices at the national level
- Create state-level GCC incentive packages tied to job creation 
  milestones
- Invest in digital infrastructure — broadband and airport capacity 
  — in high-potential cities
- Establish university-GCC partnership frameworks to accelerate 
  talent pipeline development
- Publish standardised, annually updated city-level data across the 
  25 metrics used in this framework to enable ongoing benchmarking
